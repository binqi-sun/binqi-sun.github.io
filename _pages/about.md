I am a Research Associate at the Chair of Cyber Physical Systems in Production Engineering, Technical University of Munich ([TUM](https://www.tum.de/en)), where I received my doctoral degree (summa cum laude), under the supervision of [Marco Caccamo](https://rtsl.cps.mw.tum.de/personal_page/mcaccamo/). During my doctoral studies, I was also a visiting researcher in the Department of Computer Science, University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign ([UIUC](https://illinois.edu/)), hosted by [Tarek Abdelzaher](https://abdelzaher.cs.illinois.edu/). Prior to joining TUM, I received my master's degree from the Department of Automation, [Tsinghua University](https://www.tsinghua.edu.cn/en/), where I was advised by [Ling Wang](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=lC7bVMwAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao). I also worked as a StarBridge research assistant at Microsoft Research Lab Asia ([MSRA](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/lab/microsoft-research-asia/)). 

My research lies at the intersection of real-time cyber-physical systems, optimization, and artificial intelligence. I develop methods for building intelligent systems that operate predictably, efficiently, and safely under stringent timing and resource constraints. My work spans both **real-time systems for AI**, including the scheduling and optimization of AI workloads, and **AI for real-time systems**, including learning-assisted system design and optimization. More broadly, I aim to advance the systems foundations of trustworthy embodied intelligence -- enabling intelligent agents to sense, reason, and act reliably in the physical world. 

**I am currently on the academic job market and am seeking faculty positions.** I am always happy to discuss research ideas and potential collaborations—please feel free to reach out by email.
